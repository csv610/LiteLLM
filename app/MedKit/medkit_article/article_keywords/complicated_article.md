# Case Study: Multi-Systemic Clinical Presentation

## Patient History
A 68-year-old male with a significant history of **Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus**, **Chronic Kidney Disease (Stage III)**, and **Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH)** presented to the emergency department. He reported a two-week history of worsening **dyspnea on exertion** and **peripheral edema**. His current medications include **Metformin**, **Lisinopril**, and **Tamsulosin**. He also noted regular use of **Ginkgo Biloba** for cognitive support.

## Clinical Examination
Physical examination revealed **bilateral pitting edema** in the lower extremities and **basilar crackles** on pulmonary auscultation. A grade II/VI **systolic murmur** was noted at the apex.

## Diagnostic Workup
The medical team ordered an urgent **Complete Blood Count (CBC)**, **Comprehensive Metabolic Panel (CMP)**, and **B-type Natriuretic Peptide (BNP)**. The BNP was significantly elevated at 1,200 pg/mL. A **12-lead Electrocardiogram (ECG)** showed **atrial fibrillation** with a rapid ventricular response. Subsequently, a **Transthoracic Echocardiogram (TTE)** was performed, revealing a reduced **Left Ventricular Ejection Fraction (LVEF)** of 35%, consistent with **Congestive Heart Failure**.

## Treatment and Procedures
The patient was started on intravenous **Furosemide** for diuresis. Due to his history of **atrial fibrillation**, anticoagulation with **Apixaban** was initiated. After stabilization, he underwent a **Coronary Angiogram** to evaluate for **Ischemic Cardiomyopathy**, which revealed a 90% stenosis in the **Left Anterior Descending (LAD) artery**. A **Percutaneous Coronary Intervention (PCI)** with a drug-eluting stent was successfully performed.

## Follow-up
The patient was discharged with instructions to continue his medications and follow up for a repeat **Echocardiogram** in three months. He was also advised to monitor his **Blood Glucose** levels and **Serum Creatinine** closely.
