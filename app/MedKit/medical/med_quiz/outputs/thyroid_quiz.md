**Question 1**

{'A': 'Administer levothyroxine 50 mcg daily and re-evaluate in 6-8 weeks.', 'B': 'Order a thyroid ultrasound to assess for nodule size and vascularity.', 'C': 'Initiate treatment with a high-dose steroid regimen to manage suspected Hashimoto’s thyroiditis.', 'D': 'Perform a complete blood count (CBC) and erythrocyte sedimentation rate (ESR) to evaluate for autoimmune thyroid disease.’}

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:**

*   **Why B is Correct:** This patient presents with a solitary thyroid nodule, a key feature of thyroid cancer. The *best next step* is to perform an ultrasound to assess the nodule’s characteristics (size, shape, echogenicity, vascularity). These characteristics are crucial for determining the likelihood of malignancy and guiding further investigation.
*   **Why A is Incorrect:** Levothyroxine is a thyroid hormone replacement medication. While helpful in hypothyroidism, it doesn’t address the potential malignancy of a thyroid nodule.
*   **Why C is Incorrect:** Steroids are not indicated for Hashimoto’s thyroiditis, which is an autoimmune condition. Steroids can mask the symptoms of hypothyroidism and are not a primary treatment.
*   **Why D is Incorrect:** CBC and ESR are useful in evaluating for inflammatory conditions, but they are not specific for thyroid disease and do not guide the initial management of a thyroid nodule.

**Correct Answer:** C

**Explanation:**

*   **Why C is Correct:** This patient presents with palpitations and a history of hyperthyroidism, strongly suggestive of an underlying arrhythmia. A 24-hour Holter monitor is the *best next step* to objectively assess for the presence and frequency of arrhythmias, which are common in patients with hyperthyroidism.
*   **Why A is Incorrect:** Amiodarone is a potent antiarrhythmic drug with significant side effects and is not the initial treatment for hyperthyroidism-related arrhythmias.
*   **Why B is Incorrect:** While beta-blockers can manage palpitations, they don't address the underlying hyperthyroidism and can sometimes worsen the symptoms.
*   **Why D is Incorrect:** Dietary modifications are not a primary treatment for hyperthyroidism or arrhythmias.

**Question 4**

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:**

*   **Why B is Correct:** This patient presents with acute thyroiditis, likely Hashimoto’s thyroiditis, characterized by inflammation and release of thyroid hormones. *Methimazole* is the *best initial management* to suppress thyroid hormone synthesis and reduce the inflammatory response.
*   **Why A is Incorrect:** Corticosteroids are not effective in treating thyroiditis and can mask the symptoms of hypothyroidism.
*   **Why C is Incorrect:** Referral to an endocrinologist is appropriate for long-term management, but the *initial* management involves suppressing thyroid hormone synthesis.
*   **Why D is Incorrect:** Constipation is a common side effect of hypothyroidism, but dietary changes are not a primary treatment for thyroiditis.
