# Medical Quiz: hypertension
**Difficulty:** easy

## Question 1
**A 62-year-old African-American male presents to the clinic complaining of worsening headaches, dizziness, and shortness of breath over the past month. He reports a family history of hypertension and obesity. His vital signs are: BP 160/95 mmHg, HR 88 bpm, RR 18 bpm, and SpO2 97% on room air. Physical examination reveals no remarkable findings. Initial laboratory tests show a normal serum creatinine and electrolytes. What is the MOST appropriate initial management step for this patient?**

**Options:**
A) Immediate initiation of a thiazide diuretic to rapidly lower blood pressure.
B) Prescription of an ACE inhibitor to block the renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system and reduce afterload.
C) Referral to a cardiologist for evaluation of potential cardiac involvement and initiation of lifestyle modifications including dietary changes and exercise.
D) Immediate placement of a transcutaneous continuous blood pressure monitoring device to accurately assess his blood pressure trends.

**Answer:** C

**Explanation:** The most appropriate initial management for a patient with stage 1 hypertension, particularly one with risk factors like age, ethnicity, and family history, is a comprehensive approach that includes lifestyle modifications and referral to a specialist. Option C correctly identifies this approach.  Thiazide diuretics (A) are often used but don't address the underlying causes and can have significant side effects. ACE inhibitors (B) are a reasonable choice but lifestyle modifications are paramount. The focus here is on a holistic, evidence-based approach, aligning with global hypertension guidelines which prioritize lifestyle changes and referral for further management.
